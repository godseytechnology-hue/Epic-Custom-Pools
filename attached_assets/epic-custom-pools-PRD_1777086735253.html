1. Executive Overview & Brand Strategy

Epic Custom Pools is a full-service pool design and installation company serving the Fort Worth metropolitan area and surrounding communities including Weatherford, Aledo, Granbury, Mineral Wells, Benbrook, and Hudson Oaks. The company specializes in gunite/concrete pools, fiberglass pools, and swim spas/hot tubs, serving both residential homeowners and home builders or developers.

This document defines the complete digital strategy for establishing Epic Custom Pools as the dominant online presence in its service region — from initial MVP website launch through a long-term SaaS product evolution. The primary objective is immediate lead generation while building lasting brand authority that justifies premium pricing and attracts high-intent buyers.

🎯 Core Brand Promise Epic Custom Pools is positioned as a trusted luxury partner — not a commodity contractor. Every element of the website, from copy to photography to UX, should reinforce the message: we elevate homes and lifestyles through expert craftsmanship and personalized design.

2. Differentiation & Strategic Advantages

The following opportunities represent areas where Epic Custom Pools can create a decisive competitive advantage online. Most competitors in the DFW market fail on all of these fronts simultaneously.

Highest-Impact Differentiators

🏆 Pricing Transparency

The pool industry is notorious for opaque pricing. A dedicated /pricing page with honest ranges, cost-affecting factors, and a visual cost configurator will capture buyers who are frustrated by competitors forcing them to "call for a quote." This page is projected to be among the highest-converting pages on the site and will rank for high-intent keywords like "how much does a pool cost in Texas."

🏆 B2B Builder/Developer Track

No local pool contractor has a dedicated landing page or conversion funnel for home builders. A /home-builders page targeting "pool contractor for home builders Fort Worth" is a zero-competition keyword opportunity with extremely high contract value. One builder relationship can yield 10–30 pools per year.

🏆 Hyper-Local City Pages

A hub-and-spoke local SEO architecture with individual city landing pages (Fort Worth, Weatherford, Aledo, Granbury, Mineral Wells, Benbrook, Hudson Oaks, Burleson) targets geographic searches that competitors ignore. Each page contains unique, locally-relevant content — not duplicated filler.

🏆 Free Design Consultation CTA

Offering a free design consultation as the primary CTA lowers the barrier to engagement dramatically. Buyers in a $50K+ purchase decision need a low-risk first step. This positions Epic Custom Pools as confident and service-oriented rather than transactional.

🏆 Trust Signal Architecture

Years in business, project count, certifications (APSP, BBB), and client testimonials should be surfaced on every page — not buried on an About page. Trust signals adjacent to conversion forms measurably increase form completion rates.

🏆 Mobile-First Experience

Over 70% of local service searches happen on mobile. A click-to-call button, sticky CTA bar, and fast-loading mobile layout will capture competitors' leads who have poor mobile experiences.

Every page must contain three conversion touchpoints to maximize lead capture without being aggressive:

  • Mid-page embedded form — 3-field inline form (Name, Phone, City/Zip) within the content flow
  • Sticky mobile CTA bar — fixed to bottom of screen on mobile with call and quote options

6. Lead Capture System

MVP Lead Form Configuration

The primary lead form should be intentionally minimal — the goal is to reduce friction and get the contact, not to pre-qualify extensively at the form stage.

FieldTypeRequiredRationale
Full NameTextYesPersonalization in follow-up
Phone NumberTelYesPrimary contact method for contractors
City / Zip CodeTextYesService area qualification
Pool Type InterestDropdownNoLead routing, optional to keep friction low
MessageTextareaNoOptional context, don't require

Email Notification Flow

On form submission, the system sends an immediate email notification to the contractor containing all submitted fields, the page the lead originated from, the timestamp, and the referring URL. This is implemented via a Next.js API route connected to Resend or SendGrid (both offer free tiers sufficient for MVP lead volumes).

⚡ Lead Response Time Matters Enormously Studies show that responding to a web lead within 5 minutes increases conversion by over 400% vs. responding after 30 minutes. The contractor should be coached to treat email notifications as urgent and respond same-day at minimum.

Post-Submission Experience

  • Redirect to a dedicated /thank-you page (enables GA4 conversion tracking)
  • Thank-you page includes: confirmation message, expected response time, contractor phone number, and links to gallery and pricing pages to keep engagement warm
  • Optional: auto-reply email to the prospect confirming receipt

7. Live Chat & SMS Integration

Recommended Stack: Tawk.to + Zapier + Twilio

This combination delivers real SMS alerts to the contractor's phone the moment a website visitor initiates a chat — with zero monthly cost at MVP scale.

ComponentToolCostRole
Chat WidgetTawk.toFree foreverEmbeds on site, captures visitor messages
Automation BridgeZapier Free TierFree (100 tasks/mo)Triggers on new Tawk.to chat event
SMS DeliveryTwilio~$0.008/SMSDelivers chat notification to contractor phone

Integration Setup Steps

  • Step 1: Create Tawk.to account → copy embed script → add to Next.js _app.js or layout component
  • Step 2: In Zapier, create a Zap: Trigger = "New Chat Started" in Tawk.to
  • Step 3: Action = Twilio "Send SMS" with visitor name, message, and page URL
  • Step 4: Contractor receives: "New Epic Pools chat from [Name] on /gunite-pools: [message]"
  • Step 5: Contractor can respond via Tawk.to mobile app in real time

Post-MVP Chat Upgrade Path

As lead volume grows, the chat system can evolve to include AI-powered auto-responses for after-hours inquiries, CRM integration for automatic lead logging, and chat-to-consultation booking flows — all covered in the Post-MVP section.

12. SaaS Pivot Strategy — AI Pool Designer & Estimator

🚀 The Big Opportunity The same AI tool built to help Epic Custom Pools convert leads faster can be productized and licensed to pool contractors across the United States. There are approximately 25,000–30,000 pool installation contractors in the US. Even penetrating 1% of that market at $200/month represents $600,000 in annual recurring revenue.

Product Vision

An AI-powered web application that allows a pool contractor (or their client) to:

  • Upload or describe a backyard (dimensions, slope, obstacles)
  • Select design preferences (pool type, shape, features, finish)
  • Receive an AI-generated pool design concept with visual rendering
  • Get an itemized cost estimate including materials, labor, and equipment
  • Export a branded PDF proposal the contractor can present to the homeowner

Market Sizing

SegmentAddressable UnitsPrice PointTAM
US Pool Contractors (SaaS)~27,000$150–$300/mo$48M–$97M ARR
Pool Dealers / Showrooms~8,000$200–$400/mo$19M–$38M ARR
Home Builder Partnerships~5,000$300–$600/mo$18M–$36M ARR
Total Addressable Market$85M–$171M ARR

Build Sequence (Epic Custom Pools as Pilot)

  • Phase 1: Build the cost estimator as a lead capture widget on the Epic Custom Pools website (this is a post-MVP feature that directly generates leads)
  • Phase 2: Add AI design suggestions powered by Claude or GPT-4 Vision — prospect describes backyard, gets 3 design concepts
  • Phase 3: Build branded PDF export — Epic Custom Pools logo, itemized estimate, design concept, CTA to schedule consultation
  • Phase 4: Package as white-label SaaS product — other contractors pay monthly fee to use the same tool under their own brand
  • Phase 5: Add contractor marketplace — homeowners use the tool to get matched with vetted contractors nationally

Competitive Moat

The key insight is that Epic Custom Pools becomes the reference customer and product development partner for the SaaS tool. Real usage data from actual pool sales informs the AI model's accuracy on materials and pricing — something a software-only startup couldn't replicate without industry partnerships. This creates a durable data advantage.

14. Domain Strategy & Launch Wiring

The client already has a domain. The recommended approach is to build and test the entire site on Replit's default preview URL, then wire the existing domain at launch. This separates the build phase cleanly from the go-live moment and eliminates any risk of disrupting an existing web presence during development.

Pre-MVP: Confirm Domain Access

Before Sprint 0 begins, confirm the following with the client — domain wiring at the end will stall if any of these are missing:

ItemAction RequiredWho
Domain registrarIdentify where the domain is registered (GoDaddy, Namecheap, Cloudflare, Google Domains, etc.)Client
Registrar loginConfirm client has active login credentials and DNS accessClient
Current DNS recordsCheck if domain currently points anywhere (existing site, parked, etc.) — avoid disruptionDeveloper
Email on domainIf client uses @theirdomain.com email, confirm MX records will not be overwritten at launchDeveloper
⚠️ Critical: Get Registrar Access Before You Start Building The most common launch-day blocker is a client who can't find their registrar login. Collect this information in Sprint 0, not Sprint 7.

During the Build: Replit Dev URL

Replit automatically generates a live preview URL for every project in the format https://[project-name].[username].repl.co. Use this URL for all development, testing, and client review sessions across Sprints 1–6. No domain access is required during this phase.

📌 siteConfig.js — Set Up for Easy Domain Swap On day one of Sprint 1, configure the site URL as an environment variable so all sitemap URLs, canonical tags, and Open Graph meta tags update automatically when the domain is swapped at launch — no manual find-and-replace needed.

siteUrl: process.env.NEXT_PUBLIC_SITE_URL || 'https://[project].repl.co'

At launch, simply update NEXT_PUBLIC_SITE_URL in the environment and rebuild.

At Launch: Two Deployment Options

Option A — Replit Custom Domain (Simplest)

Replit supports custom domain attachment directly in project settings. Add the client's domain, then update two DNS records at the registrar. Propagation typically completes within 1 hour. SSL is handled automatically. This is the fastest path if staying entirely within Replit.

StepActionWhere
1In Replit project settings → Custom Domain → enter client domainReplit dashboard
2Replit provides a CNAME target — copy itReplit dashboard
3Add CNAME record pointing domain to Replit's targetClient's DNS registrar
4Wait for DNS propagation (15 min – 2 hrs)—
5Verify SSL certificate issued automaticallyBrowser
6Update NEXT_PUBLIC_SITE_URL env variable to live domain and rebuildReplit Secrets panel

Option B — Deploy to Vercel (Recommended for Performance)

For better Core Web Vitals scores, higher uptime, and edge-network delivery, deploy the final build to Vercel. This is the professional production choice and remains free for this project's scale. The workflow is: Replit → push to GitHub → Vercel imports from GitHub → point client domain to Vercel.

StepActionWhere

2Create a free Vercel account and import the GitHub repositoryvercel.com

5Vercel provides DNS records — add them at client's registrar (A record + CNAME)Client's DNS registrar

🏎️ Why Vercel Wins for SEO Vercel serves assets from a global edge network, which means faster load times from anywhere in DFW. Google's Core Web Vitals — a ranking factor — are directly impacted by server response time. Vercel consistently outperforms Replit's hosted deployment on LCP (Largest Contentful Paint), which matters for the competitive pool contractor search results.

MX Record Protection (If Client Has Email on Domain)

If the client receives email at their domain (e.g. info@theirdomain.com), their DNS currently has MX records pointing to their email provider (Google Workspace, Microsoft 365, etc.). When adding a CNAME or A record for the website, do not touch the MX records. Adding the website DNS records alongside existing MX records is standard — they do not conflict. Confirm this before making any DNS changes.
